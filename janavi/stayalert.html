About Stay Alert

Being alert helps women recognize unsafe situations early and make smart decisions quickly. Whether walking alone, travelling, shopping, or using public transport, paying attention to surroundings can reduce risks and improve personal safety. Staying alert means avoiding distractions, noticing unusual behavior, and trusting your instincts when something feels uncomfortable.

Women can improve their safety by keeping emergency contacts ready, sharing live locations with trusted family members, and avoiding isolated places at night. Carrying safety tools like whistles, alarms, or flashlights can also provide confidence and protection. Awareness, confidence, and preparedness together create a safer environment in daily life.
