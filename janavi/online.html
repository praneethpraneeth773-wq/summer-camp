Protect your privacy online — safety begins with smart digital choices

About Online Security

Online security is very important for women in today’s digital world. Social media, messaging apps, and online platforms help people stay connected, but they can also expose personal information if not used carefully. Women should avoid sharing private details such as home addresses, passwords, phone numbers, or live locations publicly. Using strong passwords and enabling two-factor authentication can help keep accounts safe from hackers and misuse.

Women should also be careful while talking to strangers online and avoid clicking suspicious links or downloading unknown files. Cyberbullying, fake profiles, and online scams are common problems, so it is important to block and report harmful behavior immediately.
