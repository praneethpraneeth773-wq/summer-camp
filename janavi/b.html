Self Defense

Front Kick

A front kick is an ideal self-defense move for women because it is highly effective at neutralizing threats, quick to execute, and easy to learn. To build an effective defense, review the techniques and escape strategies outlined on Healthline, the targeted strike mechanics on Krav Maga Los Angeles, and the fundamental body mechanics detailed on Self Defense Tutorials.

How to Execute the Front Kick:

  • Drive the knee: From a balanced, neutral stance, quickly drive your dominant knee straight up toward your target's height.
  • Flex your foot: Pull your toes back and strike with the ball of your foot (the flat area right beneath your toes) to maximize the impact area.
  • Extend and retract: Straighten your leg sharply to drive the attacker backward, then immediately pull your foot back to prevent them from grabbing your leg.
